# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Casual and learning Japanese riichi mahjong players who need help turning a completed hand into a correct score. They may know tile families and common yaku, but they should not need to memorize notation, payment tables, or every scoring edge case to use the calculator confidently.

## Product Purpose

This product helps users build a riichi mahjong hand, choose the winning tile and win context, add relevant conditions such as riichi or dora, and receive yaku, fu, and payment results. Success means the user can understand what they entered, see what is missing before submission, and trust the result without feeling punished for not being an expert.

## Positioning

The calculator makes riichi scoring approachable by translating expert scoring mechanics into a visual, guided hand-building workflow.

## Brand Personality

Clear, patient, and table-ready. The interface should feel lightly connected to Japanese mahjong through the actual tile visuals and restrained supporting details, while keeping the surrounding theme quiet enough that the scoring task remains the focus.

## Anti-references

Avoid casino styling, decorative Japanese tropes, brush fonts, lanterns, temple imagery, heavy red-and-black theming, and expert-only density. Avoid relying on text tile codes such as `1m` or `3p` as the primary tile representation when real tile faces can reduce confusion.

## Design Principles

Prioritize recognition over notation. Use clear tile faces, labels, and grouping so casual users can select tiles by sight rather than decode shorthand.

Expose complexity progressively. Keep the primary hand-building flow obvious, and let advanced controls such as melds, dora, rules, honba, and riichi sticks appear where they are useful without overwhelming the first pass.

Make readiness visible. Show what the hand still needs, what has already been chosen, and why a score cannot yet be submitted.

Treat scoring output as explanation, not just answer. Present yaku, fu, limits, and payments in a hierarchy that helps users learn why the result happened.

Use Japanese character through material restraint. Let mahjong tiles, table-like structure, and careful rhythm carry the theme instead of decorative cultural motifs.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA. Tile and state choices must not rely on color alone; selected, winning, disabled, error, and success states need text, shape, icon, or position cues as well. Support keyboard navigation, visible focus states, readable labels, color-blind-friendly contrast, reduced motion preferences, and touch targets that work on mobile devices.
